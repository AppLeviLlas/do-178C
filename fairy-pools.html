Discover the Enchanting Fairy Pools

Hidden away in the lush native bush near Kerikeri lies one of the area's most magical natural attractions - the Fairy Pools. These crystal-clear emerald waters form a series of enchanting pools and small cascades that seem to have been plucked straight from a fantasy novel.

Unlike the more dramatic Rainbow Falls or the popular Charlie's Rock, the Fairy Pools offer a more intimate and serene experience. The series of interconnected pools have been carved into the volcanic rock over centuries by the gentle flow of a small stream, creating perfectly formed swimming holes of varying depths.

What makes the Fairy Pools truly special is their otherworldly beauty. The water maintains an incredible clarity and takes on a mesmerizing blue-green hue when the sun hits it just right, giving the pools their magical reputation and name. Surrounded by native ferns, mossy rocks, and dappled sunlight filtering through the canopy, it's easy to understand why local legends speak of fairies inhabiting this enchanted spot.

A visit to the Fairy Pools offers not just a swimming opportunity but a chance to connect with one of Kerikeri's most pristine natural environments. The secluded location has helped preserve the unspoiled beauty of this hidden gem.

Important Visitor Information

The Fairy Pools are a natural, undeveloped site that requires special consideration:

  • The track to the pools is unmarked and can be difficult to follow. Consider visiting with someone who knows the way or research directions carefully beforehand.
  • Wear appropriate footwear as the track involves stream crossings and can be slippery.
  • Respect this pristine environment by taking all trash with you and avoiding any damage to vegetation.
  • The pools can be deeper than they appear - always check depth before jumping in.
  • There are no facilities at the site, so bring drinking water and any other supplies you might need.
  • Consider using biodegradable sunscreen to minimize impact on the delicate ecosystem.

Finding the Fairy Pools

The Fairy Pools are somewhat off the beaten path, which adds to their mystique but can make them challenging to find:

  • The trailhead is located off Waipapa Road, approximately 10 minutes drive from central Kerikeri.
  • Look for a small unmarked parking area near the stream crossing (limited spaces available).
  • The trail follows the stream for most of the way - if you lose the path, simply continue following the water upstream.
  • The walk takes approximately 25-30 minutes each way, depending on conditions.
  • Some sections require crossing the stream, so be prepared to get your feet wet.
  • The final approach involves some scrambling over rocks - take care on slippery surfaces.
  • You'll know you've arrived when you see the distinctive emerald pools set among the rocks.

Best Times to Visit

To make the most of your Fairy Pools experience:

  • Visit on a sunny day when sunlight can penetrate the forest canopy and illuminate the pools, enhancing their magical emerald color.
  • Midday (between 11am and 2pm) offers the best lighting for seeing the pools' colors.
  • Weekdays are generally quieter than weekends, providing a more serene experience.
  • Summer and early autumn provide the most comfortable swimming conditions.
  • After moderate rain, the cascades between pools are more impressive, but avoid visiting after heavy rainfall when water can be discolored and currents stronger.
  • Allow at least 2-3 hours for the full experience, including hiking time and time to explore and swim.

The Magic of the Fairy Pools

There's something genuinely magical about the Fairy Pools that goes beyond their picturesque appearance. Many visitors describe a sense of tranquility and enchantment that permeates the area, as if stepping into another world.

The unique geological features of the site contribute to this atmosphere. The pools have been formed by water gradually eroding the volcanic rock over thousands of years, creating smooth, sculptural formations that seem almost deliberately designed. Some of the larger pools reach depths of over two meters, while others are shallow enough for gentle wading.

Local Māori traditions speak of the pools as a special place where the veil between our world and the spirit world grows thin. While no official Māori name for the site is widely used, the area has cultural significance to local iwi, who have traditionally respected it as a place of natural power.

The clarity of the water is remarkable even by New Zealand standards. On sunny days, the pools take on their famous emerald glow - a natural phenomenon caused by the mineral content of the water combined with the unique volcanic rock that forms the pools' base.

Photography Paradise

The Fairy Pools have become increasingly popular with photographers, and for good reason. The combination of crystal-clear emerald waters, smooth volcanic rock formations, dappled light filtering through the forest canopy, and small cascades creates perfect conditions for stunning nature photography.

For those looking to capture the magic of the Fairy Pools, here are some tips:

  • Visit around midday when the sun is high enough to illuminate the pools, bringing out their vibrant color.
  • A polarizing filter can help reduce glare on the water surface and enhance the visibility of the underwater features.
  • Long exposure shots work beautifully to capture the small cascades between pools, creating a silky water effect.
  • Include elements of the surrounding forest to frame your shots and provide context.
  • Look for interesting details like water patterns, light playing on the rock formations, or native plants growing alongside the pools.

Remember to respect the environment while photographing - stay on established paths where possible and avoid disturbing the natural setting for the perfect shot.
