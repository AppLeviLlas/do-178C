Understanding DO-178C

DO-178C, "Software Considerations in Airborne Systems and Equipment Certification," is the primary document by which the certification authorities such as FAA, EASA, and Transport Canada approve all commercial software-based aerospace systems.

First published in 2011 as a successor to DO-178B, this standard provides guidelines for the production of software for airborne systems and equipment that performs its intended function with a level of confidence in safety that complies with airworthiness requirements.

The standard is organized into software planning, development, verification, configuration management, quality assurance, and certification liaison processes. Each process is described in terms of objectives, activities, inputs, and outputs.

This guide aims to provide a comprehensive overview of DO-178C, its key components, and practical implementation strategies for aviation software developers and certification specialists.
