Magnificent Rainbow Falls

Rainbow Falls (Waianiwaniwa in Māori) is undoubtedly Kerikeri's most famous and spectacular waterfall. Standing at an impressive 27 meters high, this magnificent single-drop waterfall creates a perfect curtain of water that plunges into a large swimming pool below.

The waterfall derives its name from the stunning rainbows that form in its mist on sunny days, creating a magical atmosphere that has captivated visitors for generations. Part of the Kerikeri River, Rainbow Falls is not only a natural wonder but also a site of cultural significance to the local Māori people.

Located just a short drive from Kerikeri town center, Rainbow Falls is easily accessible and offers various viewing platforms that provide different perspectives of this breathtaking natural feature.

The thundering sound of water, the refreshing mist in the air, and the lush surrounding bush combine to create an immersive experience that connects visitors with the raw power and beauty of nature.

Visitor Tips

  • Visit early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds and get the best lighting for photos.
  • The path to the falls is well-maintained and suitable for most fitness levels.
  • For the best rainbow effect, visit on a sunny day between 10am and 2pm.
  • Bring insect repellent during summer months.
  • The lower viewing platform offers the best full view of the falls.
  • Swimming is possible in the pool below, but be cautious of strong currents, especially after heavy rain.
  • Combine your visit with the nearby Stone Store Basin for a perfect half-day excursion.
  • In summer, bring water and sun protection as the viewing areas can get hot.
  • Allow at least an hour to fully explore all viewing points and enjoy the surroundings.

Walking Tracks

Rainbow Falls is accessible via several walking tracks:

Rainbow Falls Track:
The most direct route from the car park, approximately 10 minutes walking time.
Kerikeri River Track:
A longer option that starts from the Stone Store Basin and follows the Kerikeri River to the falls (approximately 1 hour one way).
Rainbow Falls Loop:
A circular track that provides views from multiple vantage points (approximately 30 minutes).

All tracks are well-maintained and offer different perspectives of the falls and surrounding landscapes. Information boards along the routes provide insights into the local flora, fauna, and history.

History & Cultural Significance

Rainbow Falls has significant cultural importance to the local Ngāpuhi iwi (tribe). According to Māori tradition, the pool at the base of the falls was used for ceremonial purposes and was considered a sacred site.

The Māori name for the falls, "Waianiwaniwa," translates to "Waters of the Rainbow," highlighting the significance of the natural rainbow phenomenon that occurs in the mist created by the cascading water.

The area around the falls has witnessed important historical events, including early interactions between Māori and European settlers. The nearby Kororipo Pā (fortified village) and the historic Stone Store are testament to this rich cultural heritage.

Today, the falls are protected as part of a scenic reserve managed by the Department of Conservation, ensuring this natural treasure is preserved for future generations.
