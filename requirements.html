Requirements

Requirements are a fundamental aspect of DO-178C. They define what the software is intended to do and provide the basis for verification and validation activities.

DO-178C distinguishes between different types of requirements, including:

  • High-level requirements: These requirements describe the overall functionality of the system.
  • Low-level requirements: These requirements describe the detailed design and implementation of the software.
  • Safety requirements: These requirements address potential hazards and ensure the safety of the system.

It is essential to write clear, concise, and verifiable requirements. Requirements should be unambiguous and should be testable to ensure that they have been met.
