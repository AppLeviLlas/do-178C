Development Assurance

Development assurance is a key aspect of DO-178C. It encompasses all activities undertaken to ensure that the software meets its intended requirements and is safe and reliable.

The development assurance process includes the following activities:

  • Requirements management: Ensuring that the requirements are complete, consistent, and verifiable.
  • Design: Developing a software design that meets the requirements and is safe and reliable.
  • Coding: Implementing the software design in a programming language.
  • Testing: Verifying that the software meets its requirements and is safe and reliable.
  • Configuration management: Managing the software configuration to ensure that changes are controlled and that the software can be rebuilt if necessary.

Development assurance is an iterative process. The activities are performed throughout the software development lifecycle, and the results of each activity are used to improve the software.
