Charlie's Rock - A Local Treasure

Tucked away just minutes from Kerikeri's town center, Charlie's Rock is a beloved swimming spot that generations of locals have enjoyed. Unlike the more dramatic waterfalls in the area, Charlie's Rock features a series of small cascades flowing over smooth volcanic rocks, creating natural water slides and deep swimming pools that are perfect for cooling off on hot summer days.

This hidden gem gets its name from Charlie Puckey, a local settler who owned the land in the early 20th century. The swimming hole became a popular gathering place for families, and has retained its charm and popularity to this day. While not as visually impressive as Rainbow Falls, Charlie's Rock offers a more interactive waterfall experience where visitors can swim, slide, and jump from various rock platforms.

The setting is quintessentially New Zealand, with native bush surrounding the waterhole, creating a secluded natural amphitheater that traps the sun and enhances the swimming experience. Crystal clear waters flow year-round, although the volume varies significantly with rainfall.

For those seeking a refreshing dip in a beautiful natural setting, Charlie's Rock is a must-visit spot in Kerikeri that offers both adventure and relaxation.

Safety Information

While Charlie's Rock is a popular swimming spot, visitors should be aware of the following safety considerations:

  • Always check water depth before jumping, as levels can change after rain or during dry periods.
  • The rocks can be slippery, so wear appropriate footwear when walking around the site.
  • There are no lifeguards - supervise children closely.
  • After heavy rain, currents can be strong and the water may be discolored.
  • Be aware of underwater hazards such as submerged logs or rocks.
  • Respect the environment and other visitors by taking all trash with you.

Visitor Tips

  • Visit on weekdays or early in the morning to avoid crowds during summer months.
  • Bring a picnic - there are several grassy areas perfect for setting up lunch.
  • Waterproof shoes or sandals are recommended for navigating the rocky areas.
  • Bring sun protection as there is limited shade at the main swimming area.
  • Consider bringing a floating device for younger children or less confident swimmers.
  • The best jumping spots are on the far side of the main pool, but always check depth first.
  • Bring drinking water as there are no facilities at the site.
  • The natural water slides work best when there's good water flow after recent rain.

The path to Charlie's Rock is well-marked but can be muddy after rain. Wear appropriate footwear and be prepared for a short but potentially slippery descent to reach the swimming area.

History & Local Significance

Charlie's Rock has been a beloved local swimming spot for generations of Kerikeri residents. Named after Charlie Puckey, who owned the surrounding land in the early 1900s, this natural playground has a rich history in the community.

Local legend says that Charlie himself was fond of swimming in these pools, and eventually allowed public access to what was then his private property. The site became increasingly popular throughout the 20th century, particularly as a gathering place for local youth during summer months.

Many Kerikeri residents have fond memories of spending summers at Charlie's Rock, with each generation discovering the thrill of the natural water slides and jumping spots. What was once a local secret has now become more widely known, though it still retains much of its original charm.

Today, Charlie's Rock is managed as a public reserve, ensuring that future generations can continue to enjoy this special place just as their parents and grandparents did before them.

Swimming Experience

Charlie's Rock offers one of the most enjoyable swimming experiences in the Kerikeri area. The waterhole features a series of cascading pools, each with its own character and appeal to different swimmers.

The main swimming area is a deep pool at the base of a small waterfall, approximately 3 meters deep in the center. This is perfect for confident swimmers and provides enough depth for safe jumping from the surrounding rocks. Several natural platforms offer jumping heights ranging from 1 to 4 meters.

For those seeking a gentler experience, the upper pools offer shallower waters with natural water slides formed by the smooth volcanic rock. These are particularly popular with families and younger visitors who can enjoy the thrill of sliding down the polished rock surfaces into the refreshing pools below.

The water is typically crystal clear, allowing swimmers to see the rocky bottom, though it can become cloudy after heavy rain. The temperature is refreshing even in summer but not too cold, making it perfect for cooling off on hot days.

The surrounding rock formations create natural seating areas where visitors can relax between swims, and the native bush provides intermittent shade throughout the day.
